What Is Medicare?

Medicare is the federal health insurance plan for people 65 and older, people younger than 65 with disabilities, and people of any age with End-Stage Renal Disease. This coverage is the same throughout all 50 states. There are no 'networks' to be concerned with. As long as the provider accepts Medicare, you can be treated anywhere in the United States.

CMS(the Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services) reported that as of October 2021, there were nearly 64 million people on and eligible for Medicare. The United States Census Bureau says that by 2030, all Baby Boomers will be age 65 or older. This is a significant portion of the population. This is why it is important that Medicare and Medicare Supplements be very comprehensive and regulated, to ensure that Medicare recipients are protected.

What is Medicare Supplement Insurance(Medigap)?

People on Medicare typically enroll in a Medicare Advantage or Medicare Supplement plan(also known as a MediGap plan) to help with the costs not covered by Medicare. To enroll in a plan like this, you must be enrolled in Medicare Parts A and B. For many years, the Plan F was the most comprehensive supplement available. In 2020, CMS began to phase out the MediGap Plan F. For anyone new to Medicare as of January 1, 2020, the Plan F is no longer available to purchase. However, anyone that was ever eligible to purchase MediGap Plan F would always be able to keep their Plan F if they want or to purchase a Plan F from any company that offers it.

For those new to Medicare since 2020, the highest level of coverage available now is Plan G. This plan is very similar to the Plan F. The only difference between these two health plans is that the MediGap Plan F pays the annual Medicare Part B deductible and the Plan G does not. Also, like the Plan F, the Plan G is guaranteed renewable for life, as long as you pay your monthy premium. This is why the Plan G is the best health plan available. Initial Election Period

When you turn 65 you become eligible for Medicare Part A and Medicare Part B, as long as you have worked and paid into Medicare for at least 40 quarters, or 10 years. At this time, you Part A and Part B will become effective the first day of the month that you turn 65. If your birthday is on the first of the month, your effective dates would be the first day of the prior month. The 3 months prior to your effective date, the effective month, and the 3 months after are called your Initial Election Period. During this time you can choose to enroll in a Medigap plan, a Medicare Advantage plan and a Part D Prescription Drug plan.

During your Initial Election Period, you can select these plans without worrying about your pre-existing health conditions. There is no underwriting, and you can choose any plan offered in your area without asking health questions. These plans are optional, but if you don't choose coverage during this period, you generally have to wait until the Annual Election Period(AEP), to select a Part D Drug plan and Medicare Advantage plan, and you could be penalized for being eligible for coverage, but not having it. If you want to enroll in a Medigap plan, you would have to answer health related questions, and the insurance company could decline to issue your policy based on your medical history or certain prescription drug use. Annual Election Period

In the fall, from October 15 through December 7 is your Annual Election Period. During this time you can make a change to your Part D Prescription Drug Plan and/or Enroll in a Medicare Advantage plan. These changes would become effective the following January 1. This is a good time to call in and talk to your agent at Adkins Benefits. We can search all of the plans in your area and, based on your specific needs, recommend a plan customized to you.
